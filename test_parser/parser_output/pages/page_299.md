# COE230 - Engineering Customer Relationship Management

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: STT100

This course provides engineering students with a comprehensive and balanced review of customer relationship management (CRM) and procurement which are essential skills for practicing engineers in the UAE. The course defines what CRM is, the benefits it delivers, the contexts in which it is used, the technologies that are deployed, and how it can be implemented throughout the customer life cycle stages of an engineering or IT firm: customer acquisition, retention, and development. This focus of the course is on engineering firms providing B2B and B2C engineering services in the technology, construction, energy, healthcare, education, governance, and manufacturing sectors.

# COE240 - Environmental and Safety Engineering

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: STT100

This course introduces the students to the fundamentals of occupational environmental safety engineering and how to manage with emphasis on control of hazardous materials safety considerations in production facility design and maintenance operation of effective safety programs and system safety analysis techniques.

# COE250 - Engineering Systems Modeling and Simulation

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: CSC201

This course introduces undergraduate engineering students to measurement principles and the range of sensors and instruments used for measuring physical variables in a wide variety of domestic and industrial activities. The course starts with an introduction to measurement units and systems. Students learn about the instrument types, their performance characteristics, and identification/mitigation of measurement process errors. They also learn how to calculate different sensors output with the variable conversion methods. The course features data acquisition techniques, signal processing (analog, digital, and their interconversion), and post-processing of LabView’s instruments’ gathered data.

# RSN485 - Energy Storage

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: EEN441

The majority of renewable energy resources are intermittent in nature, and therefore, in order to ensure reliability and continuity of supply for those renewable energy systems, the use of energy storage devices is essential. This course discusses the different energy storage techniques involving electrochemical, mechanical and other emerging options, as well as the integration of those energy storage systems with the different renewable energy resources and their effect on the bulk power system, and design trade-offs. Furthermore, the environmental impacts, cost, reliabilities, and efficiencies for commercialization of bulk energy storage are also analysed.

# RSN477 - Nuclear Energy

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: MEC420

The course aims to provide students with basic knowledge of nuclear energy systems for the sustainable global environment. The purpose of this course is to study the fundamentals of heat generation, cooling, energy transport and energy conversion in various kinds of fission and fusion reactors, and to understand nuclear energy systems.

# RSN411 - Grid Integration of Renewable Energy

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: EEN441

This course introduces the students to large-scale integration of renewable generation in electricity grids. It demonstrates the influence of integrating renewable energy sources into existing power systems.
