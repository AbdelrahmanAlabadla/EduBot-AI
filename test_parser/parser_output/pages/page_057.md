# a. Program Learning Outcomes

# Core Program Learning Outcomes (CPLOs)

The core program learning outcomes are designed to achieve the program objectives.

- CPLO 1: Apply contemporary theories and concepts of communication for mass media such that students understand the pivotal role and responsibility that mass media has in contemporary society.
- CPLO 2: Write high-quality content for print, broadcast, and digital media, reflecting a thorough understanding of the writing styles required for each kind of communication mode.
- CPLO 3: Critique media content in relation to the concepts of accuracy, fairness, brevity, and clarity.
- CPLO 4: Evaluate media content based on principles of ethical reasoning for media, such as balance, impartiality, and credibility.
- CPLO 5: Produce high-quality media work independently or collaboratively with peers, using negotiation and conflict resolution skills.

# Program Concentration Learning Outcomes for Strategic Communication (PCLOs)

- PCLO 1: Apply a variety of strategic communication techniques relevant to public relations and advertising in order to effectively communicate key messages pertaining to diverse organizations while meeting media ethical standards.
- PCLO 2: Evaluate the use and effectiveness of new media technologies in current public relations and advertising practices.

# Program Mission

The Mass Communication programs mission is to advance undergraduate students’ academic, professional, and personal development through select programs of teaching, research, and public service that combine intense theoretical courses with professional preparation for the media workplaces. We aim to produce graduates who meet high-performance standards in Media Production and Strategic Communication. The department also strives to achieve recognition among professionals, media organizations/agencies, and scholars in mass communications regionally and internationally.

# Program Goals

To reach the mentioned mission, the program has the following objectives:

1. demonstrate a deep comprehension of concepts inherent in the field of media and communication in the digital age.
2. exhibit professional competencies in the field of media and communication as required by specializations within the field, such as oral and written communication proficiencies, critical thinking, decision-making and problem-solving.

# Program Concentration Learning Outcomes for Media Production (PCLOs)

- PCLO 1: Produce high-quality creative work both for fiction and non-fiction content for various media platforms, such as film and digital media, reflecting contemporary concerns; and
- PCLO 2: Utilize a variety of conceptual and technical practices in media production for different platforms in an expert fashion.
