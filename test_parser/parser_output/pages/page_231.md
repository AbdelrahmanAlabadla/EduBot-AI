# PSY410- Media Psychology

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY350

Media Psychology is the application of psychological theories, principles, and research to media and technology. Media psychology majorly deals with human interactions with media and technology; hence the course covers the use, creation, and distribution or sharing of media as well as the impact of the actions on individuals, society, and culture. The media is never independent of the environment; hence it discusses the social forces and issues also. The main objective of the course is to help individuals in the positive applications of media and technology.

# PSY305 Orientation to School Counseling

Credit Hours: 3

This course introduces the student to current school counseling models and effective practices. The focus is on practical issues faced by school counselors as they implement a school counseling program, work with students, colleagues, and families, and manage role responsibilities effectively.

# PSY350- Psychology of Creativity

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY230

This course is the study of the cognitive, affective, and social mechanisms that underlie creative behavior and cognition. Major psychological theories of creativity, including impacts from the environment, biology, and cognition, are examined in this course. The characteristics of creative people, the phases of the creative process, and the influence of emotion and motivation on creativity will all be studied by the students. The course will also address how culture, education, and technology affect creativity, along with real-world applications in the arts, innovation, and problem-solving.

# PSY311 Artificial Psychology

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: ENG200

This course provides an understanding of the discipline of artificial psychology and artificial emotion, analyzing the unified macro-model of the cross-disciplinary system architecture against the need-motivation-behavior framework. Students will examine topics like emotion in AI, cognitive computing, and human-computer interaction while gaining hands-on experience in building and evaluating artificial agents with psychological attributes. The course also addresses the ethical implications and future directions of integrating psychology with AI technologies.

# PSY400- Industrial Psychology

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY230

The course is designed to provide an elaborate overview of psychological principles, theories, and research to the work and business settings. It includes the scientific study of employees, the workplace, and business. Industrial psychologists contribute to an organization’s success by improving the workplace and the performance, satisfaction, and well-being of its people. It familiarizes the employee behaviors and attitudes through hiring programs, training programs, and feedback management.

# PSY420- Psychometrics

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY201

The course is designed to introduce the learner to the central concepts of psychological measurement, critical aspects of psychometric theories including test construction and validation. Measurement is fundamental to psychological research and its application and is inevitable for the practical application of the subject. Psychometrics includes the quantification of psychological concepts like moods, attitudes, personality, and various other cognitive abilities including memory, attention, and thinking.

# PSY430 Applied Psychology Research (Design Project)

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY240, PSY201

This course will provide the Psychology student with the opportunity to apply psychology knowledge and methods in an actual work environment. The student may develop an independent project of their own under the supervision of a faculty member. The Research Design Project consists of one semester-long course focusing on intensive independent research that culminates in a written thesis and oral presentation. Each student will attend seminars and complete a research or application project.

# PSY499- Internship (A &#x26; B)

Credit Hours: 3 (1.5 each)

Prerequisite: Completion of minimum 80CHs &#x26; Completion of Internship I

This course provides an understanding of the discipline of organizational behavior (OB) within a management perspective. OB is considered at an individual, group, and organization level. Job attitude, perception, values, and personality attributes are viewed from a management viewpoint with a consideration of motivation theories, decision making, and the notion of ethics as applied to the workplace. Issues of trust, leadership, and the conflict management process are reviewed.

# INR301: Introduction to Cross-Cultural Psychology

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: FWS211+FWS100

Cross-cultural psychology course will enable students to develop better understanding of the cultural effects on human psychology. The course will also analyze the similarities and differences in the behavior among individuals who were raised from different backgrounds. This course will further examine psychological diversity and the links between cultural norms and their behavior.

# PSY325 Psychology Careers and Applications

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: PSY 230

Careers in psychology are as diverse as the field itself. This course provides an overview of various career paths aligned with personal interests, abilities, and skills, broadening career perspectives in psychology. It will introduce the students to different career opportunities and processes, helping them explore their career options. Additionally, it will also sharpen the professional skills and competencies that are the prerequisites of success in the students’ career goals.
