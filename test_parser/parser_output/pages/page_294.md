# AIN430 - Machine Learning in Medicine

# AIN482 - Natural Language Processing

# AIN442 - Time Series Analysis

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: CEN320, AIN310

This course studies medical imaging and how it is used to build computer-aided diagnosis systems. It introduces students to functional feature extraction, connectivity feature extraction, shape feature extraction, and appearance feature extractions. Students work on a project to use the extracted features with classifiers and develop an automatic diagnosis system.

# AIN443 - Generative Deep Learning

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: AIN310

This course builds on the concepts and skills acquired in AIN310 Machine Learning and Pattern Recognition. It introduces students to concepts, techniques, and applications of generative modeling and deep learning. Topics covered include Generative Modeling, Variational Autoencoders, Generative Adversarial Networks, Text-to-Image Generation, and Autoregressive Models. Students connect theoretical concepts learned in the course to practice using hands-on laboratory experiences covering Generative Modeling Fundamentals, Image Generation with GANs, and Text Generation with Autoregressive Models. The course has a project. In this project, students work teams using PyCharm and TensorFlow to design a New Articles Topics Classification Model and communicate their experience using presentations and reports. Students will also analyze the impact of Machine Learning (ML) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) on society, the environment, and the economy.

# AIN305 - Artificial Intelligence for Engineers

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: CSC201, COE101

This course builds on the concepts and skills acquired in CSC201 Computer Programming I and COE101 Introductory to Artificial Intelligence. It introduces students to concepts, techniques, and applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Topics covered include Artificial Intelligence, Problem-solving, Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, and Natural Language Processing. Students connect theoretical concepts learned in the course to practice using hands-on laboratory experiences covering Integration of Player vs. Player GUI and Unbeatable Tic Tac Toe using Minimax Algorithm.

# AIN451 - Artificial Intelligence Engineering Design Project I

Credit Hours: 1

Prerequisite: Senior Level

This course builds on the concepts and skills acquired in courses throughout the Artificial Intelligence Engineering program. It is the first part of a two-part sequence and requires students to be at the senior level. It introduces students to concepts, techniques, and applications of comprehensive project design and development within Artificial Intelligence Engineering. Skills covered include problem definition, AI system design, data modeling, algorithm development, performance evaluation, and system integration. The centerpiece of the course remains the design project.

# AIN452 - Artificial Intelligence Engineering Design Project II

Credit Hours: 2

Prerequisite: AIN451

This course builds on the concepts, skills, and progress acquired and achieved in AIEN451 Artificial Intelligence Engineering Design Project I. It continues to engage students in concepts, techniques, and applications of Robotics and Automation. Topics covered include robotics, sensors, actuators, Robotics Operating Systems (ROS), and PID Control. Students connect theoretical concepts learned in the course to practice using hands-on laboratory experiences covering Robotics Simulation on ROS, Gazebo, and RViz, Setting up ROS on Raspberry Pi and Controlling Actuators, and Reading Sensor Data and Controlling the Robot with ROS and OpenCV.

# EEN366 - Introduction to Robotics

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: EEN365

This course builds on the concepts and skills acquired in EEN365 Control Systems. It introduces students to concepts, techniques, and applications of Robotics and Automation.

# AIN483 - Audio Processing for AI Applications

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: CEN320, AIN310

This course introduces students to audio signal processing and its applications. Audio processing is important in many AI and Machine Learning applications. Topics covered include audio signals, transforms, audio feature extraction, audio signal classification and segmentation, denoising, and temporal modeling.
