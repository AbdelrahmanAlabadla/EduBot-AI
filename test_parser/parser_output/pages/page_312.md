# CLP206 - Clinical Psychology and Mental Health

The course also explores the concepts of mental health and wellbeing, and how to attain it. Students would also be encouraged to link cultural psychology with core concepts of multiculturalism, ethical foundations, including group and identity formation, migration, stereotyping, prejudice, discrimination, and intergroup dynamics. The course concludes with an analysis of multiculturalism, diversity, equity and inclusion through a psychological lens.

# CLP104 - Introduction to Cognitive Psychology

This course will address questions in the field cognitive psychology, which involves foundational level understanding of mental and psychological processes responsible for thinking, reasoning, memory, perception, logic, mental representations, decision making, and analysis of various phenomenon. Students will also cover topics around information processing, judgements, language, attention, and consciousness. Various theories and selected lab experiments will be described and discussed.

# CLP105 - Introduction to Developmental Psychology

This course focuses on foundational theoretical concepts of development of a human being from infancy to adolescence. This would require the understanding of concepts through empirical research and establish theoretical concepts that would involve psychological, biological, conceptual, and behavioral development of the brain, cognition, mind, and body. The course would also dive into fundamentals of social, biological, physiological, and cognitive developments and changes that occur during the course of human growth process.

# CLP207 - Community Mental Health

This course explores the promotion of mental health and the prevention and management of mental illness at the community level. It emphasizes understanding mental health needs, reducing stigma, enhancing community-based care models, and engaging stakeholders in mental health promotion. Students will examine local and global practices, mental health systems, and culturally sensitive interventions within community contexts.

# CLP209 - Technology and AI in Psychology

This course dives into the foundations of the study of psychological science and its impact in the digital age. Students would discover introductory concepts in the area of cyber psychology, technology used to understand psychology, technology used to interact and interpret the behavior and functioning of the human mind.

# CLP210 - Abnormal Psychology and Mental Health Disorders

This is a foundational course to study the basics of mental disorders, its origins, diagnosis, and possible treatments. The course will cover introductory level definitions, historical course of development of the field, basics of psychopathology, and its theoretical models. Students would also discuss diagnosis and possible intervention plans of various psychological disorders.

# CLP312 - Clinical Counselling and Psychotherapy

This is an introductory course to clinical counselling and psychotherapy. This course will give students a glimpse of history, and evolution of psychological interventions. Further the course will take the students through various approaches of counselling and psychotherapy, their practice and research.

# CLP314 - Advance Clinical Research Methods (Qualitative and Quantitative)

This is an advanced level course in qualitative and quantitative research methods in psychology. The course would discuss various forms of qualitative and quantitative research methods and data analysis in used in psychological research.

# CLP315 - Clinical Cognitive Behavioral Interventions

This a foundational course in clinical cognitive behavioral interventions, where students would get to know the history and evolution of evidence-based interventions, types of cognitive behavioral interventions, their uses, and criticisms. The students would also dive into the basic theoretical concepts of the link between cognition and behavior.

# CLP390 - Designing and Submission of Clinical Research Project Proposal

This course requires the completion of a clinical research project, which the student submits carrying out clinical research, and its data analysis, eventually completing the writing a in-depth report. This course is a prerequisite course to the main project. Here the students are supervised to decide on a research project topic, followed by understanding of the basics of carrying out research, eventually leading to an ethics application and project proposal development.

# CLP480 - Internship 3: Field Internship Project

The Internship is an introductory clinical applied training in designing psychological treatment plans, and conducting various forms of clinical psychotherapy in a hospital/clinical settings. During this course, students are required to complete 200 hours of supervised clinical training with in-person patient/client contact hours.
