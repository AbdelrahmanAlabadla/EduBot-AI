# RSN426 - Future Sustainable Energy

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: RSN323

The course is intended to give the student an overview on the key aspects that will define future sustainable energy systems: energy supply, energy storage, economics and limited environmental impacts. The course will help students to realize the current and future trends in energy source investments. The course also include implementing fuzzy models to study the decision making for the selection of the best renewable energy scenario.

# RSN200 - Intro. to Renewable and Sustainable Energy Engineering

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: 0

This is an engineering introduction to renewable energy technologies and potentials. The course aims to introduce a general engineering/science audience to the basic concepts of renewable energy. The course will highlight the basics and potentials of various types of renewable energies, e.g., solar energy, photovoltaic, wind power, hydropower, biomass and waste energy, geothermal energy, and tidal energy. The course will compare the characteristics of various types of renewable energy, their potential, limitations, cost, and use. Lectures will be embedded with examples from real world applications and in-progress industrial developments.

# RSN301 - Energy Materials

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: CHE205

An introduction to the structure and properties of engineering materials and the processing routes utilized to optimize properties. All major classes of engineering materials are covered, including metals, ceramics, composites, and polymers. Emphasis on the relationships between chemical bonding, crystal structure, phase equilibria, microstructure, and properties including electrical band structures, electron excitation events and semiconductors.

# RSN404 - UAE Energy Regulations and Standards

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: EEN345

This course introduces students to the United Arab Emirates standards and regulations governing the production of electrical energy from renewable energy resources as well as the standards and regulations governing the integration of those renewable energy resources with grid. Furthermore the UAE standards and regulations governing the design of power distribution systems in residential and commercial buildings as well as the design of proper protection and backup power generation systems for emergencies are also discussed in details.

# RSN352 - Thermal Energy

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: MEC320

The course covers thermal energy generation in solar thermal and geothermal applications. The course builds up in parallel: i) the theoretical knowledge of heat transfer and its three mechanisms (conduction, convection and radiation) ii) its application in the form of set-ups for thermal energy conversion (boilers, heat exchangers, solar panels), energy storage tanks iii) the system integration of such set-ups as elements in thermal energy conversion processes (heat plants, district heating systems, solar power systems).

# RSN480B - Energy-Efficient Green Building Design II

Credit Hours: 4

Pre-requisite: RSN480A

Green buildings operate at a very high level of efficiency. Sustainable construction has gone mainstream, and will one day be the industry norm. This course introduces students to advanced design, construction, and operation of high-performance green buildings covering a breadth and depth of all aspects of the green building process, from materials selection to building systems and more. The course features the latest codes and standards, including LEED v4, and carbon accounting. It exposes students to the current thinking on economics, climate change, net zero buildings, and more, with contributions by leaders in the field that illustrate the most recent shifts in thinking and practice. The course is delivered over two terms with each part as a 2-credit hours course.

# COE202 - Engineering Ethics, Economy and Law

Credit Hours: 3

Pre-requisite: ENG200 + MTT102

This course integrates two interrelated general engineering disciplines, namely: ethics and economy. In the first part of the course, the students are introduced to ethical issues that practicing engineers may face in their professional practice. This includes a discussion of the code of ethics and responsibility of engineers, ethical theories, ethical problems-solving methods, and case studies based on real events that illustrate the problems faced by engineers. The case studies also show the effects of engineering decisions on society.
