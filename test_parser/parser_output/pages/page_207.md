# Program Goal 5:

To curate students’ practical skills in clinical counselling, psychotherapy, and hone innovative approaches to real-world clinical issues.

# Program Goal 6:

To produce graduates able to formulate sophisticated research questions and conduct clinical research, culminating in a project.

# Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

Upon successful completion of the BSc in Clinical Psychology and Mental Health program, students will be able to:

- PLO1: Demonstrate theoretical understanding of concepts in the field of General Psychology including biological, cognitive, developmental, social, mental health, wellbeing and clinical aspects and the links between them.
- PLO2: Demonstrate specialized domain-specific knowledge in the areas of Clinical Psychology, Mental Health, and Well-being.
- PLO3: Develop innovative approaches and proposed solutions to real-world clinical issues using critical thinking.
- PLO4: Diagnose mental health and psychological issues and apply diverse perspectives to their address.
- PLO5: Conduct and interpret psychometric tests and evaluations.
- PLO6: Conduct various forms of psychotherapy/counselling interventions.
- PLO7: Utilize qualitative and quantitative psychological research methodologies.
- PLO8: Formulate time-relevant research questions and conduct clinical research project.
