# Bachelor of Arts

# Program Core

# ~~Compulsory Courses~~

| GDS 101 - Principles of Game Design     | Credit Hour: 3 | In this course students will explore the fundamentals of game design, including game rules, components, and player-centric design principles, while gaining an understanding of neurological factors that influence gameplay. Through practical workshops and assignments, students will analyze various game types, develop a game concept, and learn to present their ideas effectively. The course emphasizes hands-on experience with design frameworks, such as the MDA Framework, and develops the students’ ability to structure, define, and communicate game concepts with clarity. |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| GDS 121 – Introduction to Adobe Suite   | Credit Hour: 5 | In this course, students will explore the fundamental functions and interfaces of Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Animate, equipping themselves with the skills necessary to create, edit, and animate simple visual content. The course begins with an introduction to the Adobe Suite, followed by foundational skills in Illustrator, including document creation, workspace navigation, and the use of various design tools. Students will engage in practical exercises to design logos and game objects.                                                                             |
| GDS 131 - Escape Game Project           | Credit Hour: 3 | This course introduces students to the design and development of escape games, focusing on storytelling, puzzle creation, and production. Students will work in teams to conceptualize a game theme, narration, design locations, and develop engaging puzzles. Through iterative prototyping, testing, and refinement, they will produce a fully functional escape game.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| GDS 132 – Level Design Workshop         | Credit Hour: 3 | This course offers an in-depth study of game level design. Students will learn to structure level design by evaluating game elements and considering the macro structure of levels, with an emphasis on design intentions and gameplay progression. The course concludes with the modification, enhancement, and completion of a full game prototype.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| GDS 201 – Game Mechanics                | Credit Hour: 3 | This course focuses on the essential role of game mechanics in game design, guiding students to create, explain, and justify their unique game mechanisms. Students will explore innovative mechanics from various games and engage in designing their own.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| GDS 211 – History of Video Games        | Credit Hour: 3 | This course explores the history, development, and key milestones of the video game industry, from its origins to the modern era. Students will examine the industry’s major players and technological innovations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| GDS 212 – Mobile Game Culture           | Credit Hour: 3 | This course explores the evolution of mobile video games from their inception to the present day, focusing on gameplay mechanics, technological advancements, economic models, and cultural implications.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| GDS 222 – Office Tools and Applications | Credit Hour: 1 | This course introduces students to essential office tools used in modern companies, focusing on both Office 365 and Google Workspace. Students will explore word processing with Word and Google Docs, learning about interfaces, document creation, formatting, and collaborative features.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| GDS 223 – Introduction to Unity         | Credit Hour: 2 | This introductory course on Unity provides students with a comprehensive foundation in video game development using the Unity engine. Students will familiarize themselves with the Unity interface, learn to create basic projects and scenes, and grasp essential concepts such as GameObjects, components, and C# scripting.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| GDS 231 – Board Game Project            | Credit Hour: 3 | This course offers an in-depth exploration of board game design, guiding students from the initial concept to a fully developed prototype. Students will analyze existing games, brainstorm themes, and collaborate in teams to create original game ideas.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| GDS 232 – 2D Game Design Project        | Credit Hour: 5 | In this hands-on course, students will engage in the design and development of a 2D video game prototype using the popular game engine Unity. The course emphasizes iterative development, featuring playtests to evaluate prototypes and refine gameplay mechanics, controls, and visuals.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
