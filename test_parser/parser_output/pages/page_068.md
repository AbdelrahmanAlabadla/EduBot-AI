

BACHELOR OF ARTS IN PSYCHOLOGY

# The Program Learning Outcomes – PLO

On successful completion of this program the graduate will be able to:

- PLO1: Demonstrate technical competence with regard to general psychological concepts and theories as well as the content and technologies of Psychology.
- PLO2: Apply innovative, integrative thinking and problem-solving skills, applying psychological knowledge to address contemporary global challenges.
- PLO3: Apply psychological knowledge and skills to the development of personal, professional, and societal goals, with sensitivity to cultural and environmental contexts;
- PLO4: Utilize information technology and digital tools, including computational techniques, psychometrics, and digital tools, to analyze and understand human behavior in diverse settings.
- PLO5: Evaluate psychological science, ensuring respect for individual rights and cultural diversity in psychological applications.
- PLO6: Demonstrate competencies necessary for entry-level roles across healthcare, education, industry, and counseling sectors, maintaining ethical and culturally sensitive practice.

# Program Mission

The Department of Psychology’s mission is to foster the academic, professional, and personal growth of undergraduate students through a rigorous and diverse curriculum that integrates scientific research, critical thinking, and applied learning. Our program combines foundational psychological theory with experiential learning opportunities to prepare students for careers in mental health services, education, research, and human services. We aim to produce graduates who demonstrate strong ethical awareness, cultural competence, and psychological insight. The department also seeks regional and international recognition for its excellence in teaching, student development, and scholarly contribution to the evolving field of psychology.

# Program Goals

Provided below are the specific Aim/Goals of the program:

- PG1: To develop a knowledge base of human behavior across the broad areas of psychology.
- PG2: To upscale a strong research background and understanding of the scientific foundation of psychology.
- PG3: To familiarize the applications of psychology in the professions associated with psychology and the application of psychological understanding to real world questions.
- PG4: To practice Ethical and Social Responsibility in a Diverse World.
- PG5: To promote Professional Development.
