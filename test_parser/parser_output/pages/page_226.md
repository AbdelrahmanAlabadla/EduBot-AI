# INR 320- Sectors of Global Diplomacy

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 310

This is the second course in a two-course sequence (please see INR 310) that engages students with the central role that diplomacy plays in numerous aspects of international relations. There are numbers of state and non-state actors involved in diplomacy; in varying degrees, they can affect the course of international relations. This course extends further the study of several sectors of diplomacy. It elaborates on the various categories of diplomacy that are clear and present in international relations, particularly as related to questions of security, commercial transactions, cultural exchanges, and legal ramifications.

# INR 350- International Political Economy II

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 290

This is the second course in a three-course sequence (please see INR 290 and INR 430) that introduces the essential concepts of political economy and its inextricable relationship to international relations theories. This course familiarizes students with the complex structures that inform International Political Economy (IPE) and have a profound effect on international relations. Students will learn of the intersectional relationships between trade, money, finance, debt, and power. Students will analyze and engage critically with IPE structures and their global ramifications.

# INR 402- Comparative Criminal Justice Systems

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 400

This course demonstrates the diversity of criminal justice systems that can affect international relations. Using examples from different countries/politico-cultural entities as representative, it helps students learn that cultural and political specificity of a nation-state informs the criminal justice systems. The diversity of criminal justice systems affects state and non-state actors when they find themselves encountering varied systems, causing diplomatic dilemmas and/or (mis)understanding of individual rights in a highly mobile and increasingly connected world.

# INR 420- Human Rights and Global Justice

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 300

This course introduces students to a sociological perspective in international relations, demonstrating the integrated nature of the discipline. Students will engage with the idea of universal human rights and the global dimensions of (in)justice. The course content asks questions of all structures that could, at once, undermine human rights and be the avenue to global justice, including political, economic, legal, and educational structures.

# INR 321- Public Diplomacy

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 310

This course acquaints students with public diplomacy that goes beyond the formerly and formally recognized diplomatic international relations concepts. Public diplomacy contains within its arc cultural diplomacy, advocacy diplomacy, exchanges between states, media, and public discourse. Students will gain a nuanced understanding of the active and far-reaching inter-relationships that form between state and non-state actors due to public diplomacy.

# INR 400- Theories of International Relations I

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 280

This is the first in a two-course sequence (please see INR 410) that evaluates and reframes the multifarious threads of international relations theories. The course does a deep dive into said theories that inform the interdisciplinary perspective of international relations. The aim is that students gain a nuanced understanding of the diversity of theories that inform the study of international relations.

# INR 410- Theories of International Relations II

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 400

This is the second in a two-course sequence (please see INR 400) that evaluates and reframes the multifarious threads of international relations theories. The course continues the deep dive into theories that inform international relations.

# INR 430- International Political Economy III

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: INR 350

This is the third course in a three-course sequence (please see INR 290 and INR 350) that examines the essential concepts of political economy and its inextricable relationship to international relations.

# ASC 301 - Research Report Writing

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: ENG 200

This course will focus on research writing skills, which include locating, gathering, and evaluating source materials; formulating a thesis statement from a topic and/or a hypothesis; developing arguments based on solid research methods; and documenting sources in the text, in notes, and in the bibliography. Students will also work extensively on their academic and argumentative writing skills to produce high quality research papers.

# INR 499- Internship

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: Completion of minimum 80CHs

The internship and/or practicum is the experience a student has in an entity outside of their academic institution where they bring to bear the applicability of several aspects of their learning to a work-based environment. This internship is meant to heighten the competencies of the student-learner to demonstrate an aptitude for autonomous learning, a sense of responsibility in a work environment, and noticeable growth in their self-development as accountable citizens of a globalized world and as a responsible member of the socio-cultural milieu in which they operate.

# MKT 200 - Principles of Marketing

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: ENG 200

This course is designed to introduce students to the fundamental concepts of marketing and how they are currently applied in the marketplace. It should provide a stimulating environment for each participant in which they can explore the central tasks of marketing and build on previous experiences. The module enables participants to gain familiarity with the tools/processes currently used by practicing marketing professionals in analyzing market opportunities and to apply these in different contexts.
