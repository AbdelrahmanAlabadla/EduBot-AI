

# BACHELOR OF ARTS IN INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS

# Program Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of this program the graduate will be able to:

- PLO 1 Examine the disciplinary and interdisciplinary corpus of theoretical frameworks, including innovative approaches, associated with International Relations.
- PLO 2 Construct measured arguments to support positions on ethical and sustainability issues faced by institutions of global impact.
- PLO 3 Evaluate data from various interdisciplinary sources to arrive at responsibly informed analyses in research projects related to International Relations.
- PLO 4 Critique contemporary international events using diverse knowledge and skills bases to evaluate differences in a responsible manner.
- PLO 5 Evaluate one’s responsibility as an actor and contributor to international and multinational practices.

# Program Mission

The mission of the Bachelor of Arts in International Relations (BAIR) program at Abu Dhabi University is to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the complex dynamics that govern interactions among nations, alliances, and international institutions. The program aims to cultivate critical thinking, analytical, and decision-making skills by immersing students in theoretical frameworks, case studies, and research methodologies relevant to global and regional political, economic, and socio-cultural developments. Through a multidisciplinary curriculum that draws from politics, economics, history, anthropology, mass communication, and law, the program aspires to prepare graduates who are equipped to address contemporary global challenges and contribute as responsible global citizens committed to peace, security, and sustainable development.

# Program Goals

To reach the mentioned mission, the program has the following objectives:

1. Acquire familiarity with the political, social, and economic fields and systems that govern and/or influence national priorities and international relations.
2. Assess broad knowledge of integral aspects of international and GCC affairs.
3. Identity, conduct and articulate multidisciplinary and/or interdisciplinary research pertaining to International or GCC affairs.
4. Develop skills of quick analysis and critical evaluation of complex and unpredictable international proceedings;
5. Ethically resolve critical issues based on internationally agreed upon principles between and among nation-states.
