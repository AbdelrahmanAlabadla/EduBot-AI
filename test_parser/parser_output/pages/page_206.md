# Program Overview

The BSc in Clinical Psychology and Mental Health Program at ADU is exceptional in its outlook to state-of-the-art clinical training and learning to create a meaningful impact in the field of mental health and well-being. This program will empower students to understand the interplay of thoughts, emotions, behavior and biology, and then use this knowledge for practical clinical applications. The program provides outstanding training to solve real world issues, as the students will have valuable opportunity to work with practical approaches towards developing knowledge and skill in clinical psychology and mental health, on par with the best global standards practiced across the world. The program curriculum uniquely amalgamates rich theoretical concepts, research, and practical application of the skills in clinical psychology and mental health to apply in the real world. The BSc in Clinical Psychology and Mental Health program integrates globally benchmarked content to ensure relevance, comprehensiveness, and the highest academic and clinical standards.

# Program Goals (PG):

The BSc in Clinical Psychology and Mental Health program goals are:

- Program Goal 1: To produce graduates with a comprehensive understanding of the nervous system, brain, mind, emotions, thoughts, and behaviors and their interaction.
- Program Goal 2: To provide students with cutting-edge theoretical knowledge and area skills in the fields of clinical psychology, mental health, and well-being.
- Program Goal 3: To provide professional clinical training in clinical psychology and mental health via a curriculum geared to facilitate graduate registration with specific clinical psychology associations and bodies.
- Program Goal 4: To hone student skills in psychodiagnostics and psychometric assessments.
