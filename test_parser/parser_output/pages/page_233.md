# GDS 331 – Platformer Game Project

Credit Hour: 5

Prerequisite: GDS 100, GDS 223, GDS 201

This course explores the evolution of the video game industry from the early 2000s to the 2020’s, highlighting landmark titles and significant trends that shaped the gaming landscape during that era. Students will examine the emergence of new consoles, the transition to 3D gaming, and the rise of handheld devices, alongside shifts in gameplay mechanics and audience diversification.

# GDS 311 – Current Trends in Video Games

Credit Hour: 3

Prerequisite: GDS 211

This course offers a comprehensive exploration of 3D first-person-shooter game design, guiding students through the process of creating a gameplay prototype using a video game engine. Students will begin with the conceptual design phase where they will establish core gameplay mechanics, characters, and game objectives, then engage in practical activities including the writing of pitch documents, designing game elements, and programming controls while refining their prototype through rigorous testing.

# GDS 332 – First-Person Shooter Game Project

Credit Hour: 5

Prerequisite: GDS 232, Co-Req GDS 312

This course covers the influence of technology on FPS game design, from the advent of 3D engines to the integration of online communities, as well as the contrasting approaches of certain franchises. Through discussions, assignments, and analysis, students will gain a comprehensive understanding of how FPS games have shaped and continue to influence the gaming industry.

# GDS 490 - Capstone Project

Credit Hour: 6

Prerequisite: GDS 332, GDS 232, GDS 421

This course offers students the opportunity to apply their accumulated knowledge and skills in game design by developing a comprehensive capstone project in groups. Students will choose to create either a PC or Mobile game, engaging in all stages of game development from concept inception to final production. The course emphasizes iterative design, technical proficiency, creative problem-solving as well as teamwork.

# GDS 431 - Mobile Game Project

Credit Hour: 5

Prerequisite: GDS 212, GDS 201, GDS 202

This course is designed to guide students through the entire process of mobile game development, from initial concept to prototype production using a specialized game engine. Students will engage in team-based projects, where they will develop core gameplay mechanics, create compelling characters, and define the mobile game’s context and objectives.

# GDS 202 – Gamer Profiles and Behaviors

Credit Hour: 3

This course provides an understanding of players, focusing on their motivations, play styles, economic behavior, and retention. Students will be introduced to various player profiles and will engage in advanced player profile analysis.

# GDS 203 – Game Documents

Credit Hour: 3

Prerequisite: GDS 101

This course introduces students to essential game development documentation, focusing on maintaining comprehensive records. Students will conceptualize a game, targeting audience, genre, and core gameplay, and create a streamlined concept document.

# GDS 301 – Narrative Design for Video Games

Credit Hour: 3

Prerequisite: GDS 101

This course provides an introduction to narrative design, covering its development from tabletop role-playing games to modern video games. Students will practice thematic dialogue writing, minimalist storytelling, and environmental narrative techniques.

# GDS 321 – Introduction to 3D Modeling

Credit Hour: 3

This course covers the essentials of 3D modeling using industry standard software such as Blender, 3DMAX or another industry standard software. Students will be introduced to several concepts and tools such as the x, y, z axes, the types of views and the 3D tool interface.

# GDS 302 – Game Analytics and Philosophies

Credit Hour: 3

This course enhances students’ understanding of analytical tools and concepts in game design. Through practice sessions, micro challenges, and case studies, students learn data mining, analytics, and machine learning, exploring data types in games and practical applications like balancing and retention.

# GDS 312 – Game Genre Analysis (FPS)

Credit Hour: 3

This course explores the first-person shooter (FPS) genre, tracing its evolution from early titles to modern games. Students will learn to integrate and manipulate game elements using Blueprints.

# GDS 499 – Internship

Credit Hour: 3

Prerequisite: Completion of 90 Credit hours

The internship course is specifically designed to provide students an opportunity to understand industrial practices and culture before they graduate. Students will gather invaluable experience and will have the option of impressing upon their future employers.
