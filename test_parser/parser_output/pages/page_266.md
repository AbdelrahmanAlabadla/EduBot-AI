# Course Descriptions

# SWE 201 - Structured Programming

The main objective of this course is to provide students with the logic and tools required to develop structured software programs in Java. Java is a challenging programming language that is based on object-oriented programming methodology. However, this course focuses on structured programming as the main objective of learning. It also serves as a preliminary foundation for learning the object-oriented programming methodology. The material for this course includes Introduction to Computers and Java Programming, Control Structures (loops and conditions), Functions, Arrays, and Strings and the notion of algorithms for solving problems.

# CSC 301 - Data Structures and Algorithms

This course builds on the prerequisites programming courses and provides the students with an opportunity to further develop and refine their programming skills. In particular, the emphasis of this course is on the organization of information, the implementation of common data structures such as lists, stacks, queues, trees, and graphs, and techniques of data abstraction, including encapsulation and inheritance. We will also explore recursion, hashing, and the close relationship between data structures and algorithms. Operationally, applications of data structures to searching and sorting algorithms will be incorporated into programming assignments as will complexity analysis. Hands-on programming is a central component of this course.

# Data Communications Fundamentals

This course provides an introduction to modern data communications and computer networks. It presents the protocols of the physical, data link control, network, and transport layers, for local and wide area networks. The course examines in detail analog and digital signaling, analog and digital conversions, data link control, detection &#x26; correction, multiplexing, local area networks (LANs), circuit switching, packet switching, network protocols &#x26; standards, and error.

# ECT 200 - Introduction to Computing

This course is meant to be an introduction to a variety of topics in the fields of information technology and computer science. The course demonstrates the importance of computers in our day-to-day life and the kind of challenges ahead. Topics that are covered include the computer systems components, the operating systems and applications software, the importance of networking, systems analysis, databases, and software development are highlighted.

# ITE 390 - Computer Ethics

This course examines the ethical issues arising from advances in Information Technology and the responsibility that IT professionals and users have in regard to ethical computer usage. Topics covered are social impact of computing, computer crime, software theft, privacy, intellectual property rights, autonomy, technology at the workplace, technology and jobs, computer games, big data and AI, as well as new and emerging ethical issues related to technology and information.

# Degree Requirements

# COE 102 - Introductory Big Data Analytics

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisite: STT100

This course provides a general introduction to Data Analytics. It provides an essential guide to understanding and using data analytics in real-life applications without the need for any previous familiarity with programming. The course starts by introducing the main concepts of Data Analytics to provide a solid understanding of the field, its subfields, and major application areas. Students will learn the different types of data, data sources and data use, and technologies for Big Data.

# ITE 499A Capstone Design Project I

Credit Hours: 1.5

Prerequisite: Senior Level + SWE 401 + ITE 421

The objective of this course is to provide guided experience in wide areas of Information Technology to student teams working on capstone projects. The projects will integrate various engineering skills into operational prototypes. The projects will emphasize problem definition, design conceptualization, modeling, development testing, and system integration.

# ITE 399A - Internship/Project in IT-Part A

Credit Hours: 1

Prerequisite: 60 Credit Hours

# ITE 399B - Internship/Project in IT-Part B

Credit Hours: 1.5

Prerequisite: 90 Credit Hours, ITE499A

This course focuses on getting the student practically involved in the day-to-day business events in a relevant, modern and automated organization. The student will follow a well-planned course of tasks during the period of training. The plan will be devised jointly by the site-supervisor and college-supervisor.

# ITE 499B Capstone Design Project II

Credit Hours: 2

Prerequisite: ITE499A

The course will be a breakthrough in exposing the students to the professional work culture and conduct of business complexities. During the period of internship, students will develop their abilities and skills through performing required tasks.
